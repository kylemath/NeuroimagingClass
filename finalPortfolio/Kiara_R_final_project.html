Overview

I decided to expand my BrainImation visualization by keeping attention and meditation

measures and using EEG features to map these states onto an evolving weather environment.

High attention is characterized by increased beta activity relative to alpha, reflecting higher

attention and arousal. During these states, the visualization transitions into storm conditions,

including stronger wind, rainfall, and lightning, representing increased mental effort and

stress-related activation. In contrast, high meditation is associated with increased alpha activity

and reduced beta activity, reflecting relaxed states. When meditation levels increase, the

environment becomes calmer and brighter, with clouds moving slower and a visible sun glow.

Additionally, increased theta activity, commonly linked to drowsiness and deep relaxation,

introduces fog that reduces visibility and softens motion. This visualization provides

neurofeedback that allows people to perceive fluctuations in attention, stress, and relaxation in a

creative way through linking beta activity to storm conditions and alpha activity to calm weather

conditions.
