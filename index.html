Neuroimaging and Neurostimulation PSYCH 403A1 — Course Navigation

This course covers historical, contemporary, developing, and future technologies in neuroimaging and neurostimulation from both an engineering perspective on the equipment, and also a data analysis perspective. Students will get experience collecting and analyzing their own data.

Course Information

Instructor: Kyle Mathewson
Time: Mondays 9:00-12:00 PM
Location: T B-05 - Henry Marshall Tory Building
TAs: Tamari Shalamberidze (shalambe@ualberta.ca)

Prerequisites: PSYCH 213 or STAT 151 or 161 and a 300-level psychology course

Course Materials

Syllabus & Policies: Course syllabus with grading and policies.

Equipment: 25 portable EEG systems (Muse), campus MRI facility, cutting-edge fNIRS and optical brain imaging system, tDCS and TMS systems
